# THE SECOND SON OF THE HOUSE OF BELLS
## Full-Arc Summary for Reader Panel

This document contains chapter summaries, opening/closing passages,
and key dialogue for all 1 chapters. Total novel: 504 words.

PREMISE: In Cantamura, a city where law is sung into binding through
specific musical intervals, 14-year-old Cass Bellwright can hear when
someone is lying -- a quarter-tone between F and F-sharp that causes
him physical pain. His older brother Perin has been bound to service
in the House of Corda for 10 years through a contract their father
allowed. The bells their family maintains contain a secret: a question
("Do you consent to be bound?") embedded in the sub-harmonics by the
city's founder 200 years ago. No one has ever heard it. No one has
ever answered. Every binding in Cantamura is technically void.

### Chapter 1 (504 words)
**Summary:** Kaelen jumps onto a moving transport track in the elite area of Ostrum. He steals vials of glowing liquid using his bone-softening ability to break a lock. He escapes guards, leaping down toward the slums with the stolen goods as alarms wail.

**Opening:** Kaelen clung to the underside of the calcified transport track, eighty feet above the Ivory Ward's main artery. His breath plumed white in the cold air of the upper Spine. Below him, the elite of Ostrum moved in silk and fur, illuminated by crystal streetlamps burning pure marrow-oil. Ozone and cold bronze drifted up, missing the rotting-meat stink of the lower ribs. He focused on the track's vibration. The mechanical tremor running through the porous bone structure built to a steady hum. Kaelen pressed a hand against his own sternum. He felt the fine, white lines branching across his skin, tracing the network of weakened bones beneath. An ache settled deep in his joints. Elara’s cough was worsening, her knuckles swelling darker with Marrow-Rot. The transport appeared around the curve of the vertebra, a heavily armored carriage suspended from the track by ossified clamps. Two guards rode the rear platform, their hands resting on kinetic clubs. They stood loose. As the carriage passed directly over him, Kaelen released his grip on the track, dropping onto the transport's roof. The impact jarred his shoulder, a metallic tang flooding the back of his throat. **Closing:** ...the transport swaying. Kaelen scrambled up, kicking the hatch open and pulling himself back onto the roof. The two exterior guards were already climbing up the sides of the carriage. "Low-G! He's—the frequency! He used it!" one yelled. Kaelen ran to the edge of the roof and jumped. He plummetted toward the thoroughfare. At the last possible second, he caught the edge of a protruding rib-strut. The sudden stop wrenched his weakened arm. The transport screeched to a halt above him, alarms beginning to wail. Kaelen pulled himself onto the strut, breath tearing in his throat. He clutched the satchel to his chest, feeling the heat of the vials through the leather. He began the climb down toward the Rib Slums. The bone beneath his hands felt unusually warm. A deep, rhythmic vibration rose from the darkest parts of the skull and spine. *Thump. Thump.* A slow, massive heartbeat resonated through the skeleton of Ostrum.
