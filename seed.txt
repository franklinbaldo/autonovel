1. THE DUST THAT REMEMBERS
HOOK: In a city carved into the bones of a dead leviathan, a memory-thief steals a recollection that proves the beast is waking up.
WORLD: Oakhaven is a vertical city built entirely within the calcified ribs and skull of a colossal, ancient sea creature. The "ground" is a spongy marrow-swamp. Travel is via bone-dust elevators and marrow-wood bridges. The air smells of salt, old calcium, and burning marrow-oil.
MAGIC/COST: Osteomancy (Bone-singing). Practitioners can reshape bone and extract memories stored within marrow. COST: Every time a bone-singer shapes bone, their own skeleton becomes slightly more brittle, and extracting memories causes them to lose fragments of their own past. Overuse leads to "The Shattering," where a singer's bones turn to glass.
TENSION: Personal: Kaelen, a skilled marrow-thief, wants to steal enough memories to buy his sister a cure for the marrow-rot before she dies. Cosmic: He steals a memory from a high priest that shows the Leviathan isn't dead—it's hibernating, and the city's mining of its marrow is waking it up.
THEME: The cost of exploiting the foundation you live on vs. the necessity of survival. Do you let your city fall, or do you let your loved ones die?
WHY IT'S NOT GENERIC: It centers on a bizarre, biological setting (a city inside a skeleton) with a highly specific, high-cost magic system tied directly to memory and physical degradation.
