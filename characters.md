# Character Registry

## Kaelen (Protagonist)
**Role:** Marrow-Thief, desperate brother
**Proactivity:** 9
**Likability:** 6
**Competence:** 8
*   **Wound/Ghost:** His mother sold her memories to buy marrow-oil and died a Hollow, forgetting Kaelen entirely before she passed.
*   **Lie:** "If I stay strong enough and steal enough, I can protect my sister from the same fate." (He believes survival is purely transactional).
*   **Want:** To steal enough pure marrow-oil to buy a cure for his sister's Marrow-Rot.
*   **Need:** To realize that clinging to the past (and sacrificing his own memories/bones) is destroying him, and that true strength is in accepting the present and fighting the system, not just surviving it.
*   **Dialogue Distinctiveness:** Terse, direct, and focused on the immediate. Uses street slang ("brittle," "slag"). Never uses contractions when he's angry or focused. Sentences are short, clipped, like snapping twigs.
*   **Speech Example:** "I don't care about the risk, Jax. The oil is clean. The rot is spreading to Elara's joints. I am going to the Deep Core tonight, and you are going to cover me. Or I am breaking your legs."

## Elara (Secondary Protagonist)
**Role:** Kaelen's younger sister, afflicted with Marrow-Rot
**Proactivity:** 4
**Likability:** 9
**Competence:** 5
*   **Wound/Ghost:** Watching Kaelen sacrifice his own memories and bone density to keep her alive.
*   **Lie:** "I am a burden, and Kaelen would be better off if I just let the rot take me."
*   **Want:** To ease Kaelen's burden and stop him from becoming a Hollow or a Shattered.
*   **Need:** To understand her own value and fight for her life, proving to Kaelen that she is worth saving and that they must rely on others, not just themselves.
*   **Dialogue Distinctiveness:** Soft-spoken, observant, and oddly poetic for a slum child. Uses metaphors related to decay and light. Often asks questions instead of making statements.
*   **Speech Example:** "Does it hurt, Kael? When you pull the memory out? Sometimes I think you look at me like a stranger. Like you're trying to remember why you're fighting."

## Lord Vane (Antagonist)
**Role:** Head of House Vane, controller of the Deep Core
**Proactivity:** 8
**Likability:** 2
**Competence:** 9
*   **Wound/Ghost:** Born with incredibly weak bones due to his mother's overuse of Osteomancy during pregnancy. He resents his physical frailty and overcompensates with ruthless control.
*   **Lie:** "Power is the only currency that matters, and sentimentality is a weakness that will get you killed in this city."
*   **Want:** To maintain House Vane's absolute monopoly on marrow-oil and memory-wines, securing his legacy forever.
*   **Need:** (Flat arc) He needs to realize that his control is an illusion, and the Leviathan is waking up to destroy them all, but he refuses to believe it until it's too late.
*   **Dialogue Distinctiveness:** Extremely verbose, formal, and condescending. Uses long, clause-heavy sentences. Never raises his voice. Uses terms of endearment as insults ("my dear boy," "little thief").
*   **Speech Example:** "You see, Kaelen, the beauty of this city is not in its architecture, but in its absolute, undeniable fragility. You steal a drop of oil and think yourself a revolutionary. I own the very marrow of this god. You are merely a flea on a corpse."

## The Archivist
**Role:** Leader of the Hollow Syndicate, memory broker
**Proactivity:** 7
**Likability:** 5
**Competence:** 8
*   **Wound/Ghost:** She was a minor noble who was betrayed and had her most precious memories forcibly extracted and sold.
*   **Lie:** "Identity is fluid. I can be anyone I want to be if I just consume enough memories."
*   **Want:** To collect every unique memory in Ostrum, building a mosaic identity to replace the one she lost.
*   **Need:** To accept that she cannot rebuild her original self and must forge a new identity based on her actions, not stolen pasts.
*   **Dialogue Distinctiveness:** Unsettling. She shifts registers constantly because she's speaking with the memories of a hundred different people. She might start a sentence sounding like a hardened sailor and end it sounding like a frightened child. Often refers to herself in the plural ("we," "us").
*   **Speech Example:** "We remember the smell of the salt-storms, yes. We remember the feeling of a blade between our ribs. You want the deep oil, marrow-thief? We can arrange it, but the price is a memory of your mother. Just a sliver. A smile. A song she sang."
