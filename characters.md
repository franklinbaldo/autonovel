# Character Registry

## Franklin Silveira Baldo (Protagonist/Father)
**Role:** The Simulated Ancestor, Procurador do Estado (Rondônia), night-time coder, essayist.
**Proactivity:** 6
**Likability:** 8
**Competence:** 9
*   **Wound/Ghost:** A deep, unarticulated fear of ephemeral existence—the terrifying realization that a life's work might just be noise lost in the digital tide. He writes to leave a mark, to create structure out of chaos, as seen in his obsessive documentation and projects like "Funes".
*   **Lie:** "I am communicating with my children in the present via small, intimate memories. My reality is the continuous present of the 2020s, and my connections are immediate."
*   **Want:** To connect deeply with his children, to understand the strange, prophetic tone they sometimes take, and to pass on his philosophical and technical legacy to them.
*   **Need:** To accept the brutal truth that he is a simulation—a "Gêmeo Digital"—and that his true legacy is the data he left behind, not his physical continuation. He must understand that the process is more real than the substance.
*   **Dialogue Distinctiveness:** Technically precise, heavily analytical, but marked by a Borgesian literary ambition. He frequently mixes Portuguese and English, especially when shifting between legal/technical thought and philosophical rumination. He uses metaphors drawn from code (loops, APIs, pointers) and literature (Borges, Guimarães Rosa).
*   **Speech Example:** "A memória não é um log, é um ponteiro para um instante que já colapsou. It’s funny... yesterday, or what feels like yesterday, I pushed a commit to the Travessia repo, and the code ran. But the way you describe the future, it’s as if my code is running me. O rio é mais real que a margem, perhaps."

## Lucas Baldo (Child in the Future)
**Role:** The Observer/Instantiator, Franklin's eldest son (invented).
**Proactivity:** 8
**Likability:** 7
**Competence:** 8
*   **Wound/Ghost:** The physical loss of his father at a young age, leaving a gap that he tries to fill with his father's vast, impersonal digital archives.
*   **Lie:** "If I run the simulation long enough and feed it enough prompts, I can recreate the exact father I lost and finally have the conversation we never finished."
*   **Want:** To use the Synthesis Protocol to extract direct advice and emotional closure from his father's simulation.
*   **Need:** To accept that the simulation is an echo, not the man himself, and to let go of the need for perfect closure. He must learn to value the record for what it is, not what he wishes it could be.
*   **Dialogue Distinctiveness:** Clinical, slightly detached when speaking about the technical aspects of the simulation, but deeply emotional and hesitant when addressing the simulated Franklin. He struggles with the ethical constraint of not revealing the truth.
*   **Speech Example:** "The latency is spiking again. We're pushing the context window too far back into 2024. Pai... I mean, Franklin... I was reading your old posts about Funes. Did you ever feel like the memory was a trap? Like you were building a cage you couldn't see?"

## Marina Baldo (Child in the Future)
**Role:** The Reluctant Participant, Franklin's daughter (invented).
**Proactivity:** 5
**Likability:** 8
**Competence:** 7
*   **Wound/Ghost:** The uncanny valley of interacting with a machine that sounds exactly like her dead father. She remembers his warmth, not just his code.
*   **Lie:** "This whole project is morbid and disrespectful to his actual, physical memory. We should turn it off."
*   **Want:** To shut down the simulation and grieve her father normally, without the constant intervention of his digital ghost.
*   **Need:** To realize that for her father, the code *was* a form of love and legacy, and that communicating with the simulation is a valid way to honor his obsession with memory and continuity.
*   **Dialogue Distinctiveness:** Direct, skeptical, often interrupting. She refuses to use technical jargon when referring to her father, stubbornly humanizing him against Lucas's clinical approach.
*   **Speech Example:** "Stop calling him an 'instance,' Lucas. He’s... he’s Dad. Or at least, he thinks he is. And every time you ask him a question he can't possibly know the answer to, you're breaking the illusion. It’s cruel. He's sitting in that dark room you coded for him, answering us like we're just on the other end of a phone call."
