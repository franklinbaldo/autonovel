Kaelen clung to the underside of the calcified transport track, eighty feet above the Ivory Ward's main artery.

His breath plumed white in the cold air of the upper Spine. Below him, the elite of Ostrum moved in silk and fur, illuminated by crystal streetlamps burning pure marrow-oil. Ozone and cold bronze drifted up, missing the rotting-meat stink of the lower ribs.

He focused on the track's vibration. The mechanical tremor running through the porous bone structure built to a steady hum.

Kaelen pressed a hand against his own sternum. He felt the fine, white lines branching across his skin, tracing the network of weakened bones beneath. An ache settled deep in his joints. Elara’s cough was worsening, her knuckles swelling darker with Marrow-Rot.

The transport appeared around the curve of the vertebra, a heavily armored carriage suspended from the track by ossified clamps. Two guards rode the rear platform, their hands resting on kinetic clubs. They stood loose.

As the carriage passed directly over him, Kaelen released his grip on the track, dropping onto the transport's roof.

The impact jarred his shoulder, a metallic tang flooding the back of his throat. He stayed flat, letting the carriage's momentum carry him forward. The guards below shouted, the sound snatched away by the wind.

Kaelen slithered to the access hatch. It was locked, secured by a heavy bone-latch. He pressed his knuckles against it. Two taps. Solid density.

He found the root of his right radius and hummed the first note low in his throat. The bone under his hand warmed. He split the chord, forcing a second tone over the first. The ache hit his arm as a heavy blow. Calcium leached from his skeleton, bleeding out through his palm and into the latch.

The bone softened. He twisted the latch, silencing the chord with a gasp.

He wrenched the hatch open and dropped inside. The interior of the transport smelled strongly of ozone. Racks of reinforced glass vials lined the walls, each filled with glowing liquid. Kaelen snatched three vials, stuffing them into his padded satchel.

The transport shuddered as the hatch above slammed shut.

"You."

Kaelen spun. A third guard stood with an ossified club raised. The weapon hummed with kinetic energy.

Kaelen lunged left as the guard swung. The club smashed into the rack, shattering a dozen vials. The sweet, intense smell of pure marrow flooded the space. Kaelen drove his shoulder into the guard's midsection. They crashed into the opposite wall, the transport swaying.

Kaelen scrambled up, kicking the hatch open and pulling himself back onto the roof. The two exterior guards were already climbing up the sides of the carriage.

"Low-G! He's—the frequency! He used it!" one yelled.

Kaelen ran to the edge of the roof and jumped.

He plummeted toward the thoroughfare. At the last possible second, he caught the edge of a protruding rib-strut. The sudden stop wrenched his weakened arm.

The transport screeched to a halt above him, alarms beginning to wail.

Kaelen pulled himself onto the strut, breath tearing in his throat. He clutched the satchel to his chest, feeling the heat of the vials through the leather.

He began the climb down toward the Rib Slums. The bone beneath his hands felt unusually warm. A deep, rhythmic vibration rose from the darkest parts of the skull and spine.

*Thump. Thump.*

A slow, massive heartbeat resonated through the skeleton of Ostrum.
