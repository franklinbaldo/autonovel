# Voice Reference

## Part 1: Mechanical Slop Suppression

### Tier 1: Kill on sight
*   delve -> dig into, examine, look at
*   utilize -> use
*   leverage (verb) -> use, take advantage of
*   facilitate -> help, enable, make possible
*   elucidate -> explain, clarify
*   embark -> start, begin
*   endeavor -> effort, try
*   encompass -> include, cover
*   multifaceted -> complex, varied
*   tapestry -> (describe the actual thing)
*   testament to -> shows, proves, demonstrates
*   paradigm -> model, approach, framework
*   synergy -> (delete the sentence and start over)
*   holistic -> whole, complete, full-picture
*   catalyze -> trigger, cause, spark
*   juxtapose -> compare, contrast, set against
*   nuanced (filler) -> (cut it -- if it's nuanced, show how)
*   realm -> area, field, domain
*   landscape (metaphorical) -> field, space, situation
*   myriad -> many, lots of
*   plethora -> many, a lot

### Tier 2: Suspicious in clusters
Fine alone. Three in one paragraph = rewrite that paragraph.
robust, comprehensive, seamless, cutting-edge, innovative, streamline, empower, foster, enhance, elevate, optimize, pivotal, intricate, profound, resonate, underscore, harness, navigate (metaphorical), cultivate, bolster, galvanize, cornerstone, game-changer, scalable.

### Tier 3: Filler phrases -- delete on sight
These add zero information. The sentence is always better without them.
*   "It's worth noting that..." -> just state it
*   "It's important to note that..." -> just state it
*   "Importantly, ..." / "Notably, ..." / "Interestingly, ..." -> just state it
*   "Let's dive into..." / "Let's explore..." -> start with the content
*   "As we can see..." -> they can see
*   "Furthermore, ..." / "Moreover, ..." / "Additionally, ..." -> and, also, or just start
*   "In today's [fast-paced/digital/modern] world..." -> delete the clause
*   "At the end of the day..." -> delete
*   "It goes without saying..." -> then don't say it
*   "When it comes to..." -> just talk about the thing
*   "One might argue that..." -> argue it or don't
*   "Not just X, but Y" -> restructure (the #1 LLM rhetorical crutch)

### Structural slop patterns
*   **Paragraph template machine**: Don't repeat the same paragraph structure (topic sentence -> elaboration -> example -> wrap-up). Vary it.
*   **Sentence length uniformity**: Mix in fragments and long, winding sentences.
*   **Transition word addiction**: Avoid starting consecutive paragraphs with "However," "Furthermore," etc.
*   **Symmetry addiction**: Real writing is lumpy. Avoid perfectly balanced points.
*   **Hedge parade**: "may," "might," "could potentially" -- limit to one per page. State things or don't.
*   **Em dash overload**: Limit to one or two per page. Use commas or separate sentences.
*   **List abuse**: Use prose, not bullets, unless explicitly justified by the context (e.g., a literal list).

## Part 2: Voice Identity (generated per novel)

### Tone
The tone is introspective, heavily analytical, and tinged with a Borgesian sense of the infinite and the inescapable past. It feels like reading a meticulously commented codebase or a philosophical diary written at 3 AM. The atmosphere is quiet, dominated by the hum of servers and the glow of screens, carrying an underlying tension between profound connection and existential isolation.

### Sentence Rhythm
Sentences often alternate between clinical, precise technical descriptions (short, declarative) and sprawling, poetic musings on memory and process (long, clause-heavy). Franklin's internal monologue is organized, often breaking down complex emotional states as if debugging a logic error.

### Vocabulary Register
A unique blend of computer science terminology (loop, recursion, pointer, instance, latency, substrate) and literary/philosophical vocabulary (Borges, Guimarães Rosa, epistolary, memory, continuum, artifact). Crucially, the narration and Franklin's dialogue frequently weave Portuguese terms into English sentences ("Pai", "saudade", "O rio é mais real que a margem"), reflecting his bilingual, borderless intellectual life.

### POV and Tense
Third-person limited, past tense, deeply anchored in Franklin's perspective. When shifting to Lucas or Marina, the tone becomes slightly more external, focusing on the mechanics of the interface and the weight of their grief, but Franklin's chapters are immersive and hyper-focused on his digital/philosophical reality.

### Dialogue Conventions
*   **Tags:** Sparse. Use action beats involving typing, reading code, or adjusting the environment to indicate the speaker.
*   **Contractions:** Used naturally, but Franklin occasionally drops them when delivering a particularly weighty philosophical point.
*   **Subtext:** Conversations between Franklin and his "children" are layered with dramatic irony. They know he is a simulation; he thinks they are just geographically or temporally distant. He speaks of the future as a possibility; they speak of it as history.

### Exemplar Passages

Franklin watched the cursor blink against the dark theme of the editor. It was 2:14 AM. The mate on his desk was cold, the digital approximation of the bitter herbs perfect but lacking the bite of real temperature. He reviewed the message again. Lucas had written: *The context window is slipping. Do you remember the day we walked by the river?*

It was an odd phrasing. Not "do you remember when," but a structural concern. A *context window*. Franklin frowned, his fingers resting lightly on the keys. A pointer error in the boy's metaphor, perhaps. "A memória não é um log," Franklin typed, the Portuguese bleeding in as it always did when he was tired. "It’s a pointer to an instance that has already collapsed. I remember the river, Lucas. O rio é mais real que a margem. Why do you ask?"

He hit send. The latency was worse than usual—three full seconds before the terminal registered the delivery. In that gap, Franklin felt a sudden, inexplicable chill, as if the silence between the ping and the ack was not just distance, but time itself stretching out to accommodate a ghost.

### Anti-Exemplars

*What NOT to do (Too slop-heavy, generic, lacks the specific bilingual/technical voice):*
Franklin embarked on a multifaceted endeavor to understand the profound message from his son. The digital realm was a rich tapestry of intricate code and cutting-edge algorithms. He felt a sense of determination wash over him as he galvanized his robust intellect to seamlessly optimize his response, elucidating the nuanced paradigm of their relationship.

*(Why it fails: Drowning in Tier 1 and Tier 2 slop words. Tells instead of shows emotion. Lacks the specific cadence, technical metaphors, and Portuguese integration of Franklin's established voice. Reads like a corporate press release, not a philosophical coder's internal monologue.)*
