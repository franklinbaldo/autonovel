Kaelen clung to the underside of the calcified transport track, eighty feet above the Ivory Ward's main artery.

His breath plumed white in the cold, clean air of the upper Spine. Below him, the elite of Ostrum moved like bright insects in their silk and fur, illuminated by crystal streetlamps burning pure marrow-oil. It smelled like wealth up here—sterile, sharp, and entirely devoid of the rotting-meat stink of the lower ribs.

He ignored the view, focusing his attention on the track's vibration. The hum was building, a mechanical tremor running through the porous bone structure he clung to. The House Vane transport was coming.

Kaelen pressed a hand against his own sternum. He could feel the fine, white lines branching across his skin, tracing the network of weakened bones beneath. The physical cost of his past Osteomancy pulsed, a hollow ache that settled deep in his joints. He forced the sensation away. Elara’s cough was worsening, her knuckles swelling darker with Marrow-Rot. He needed the pure oil.

The transport appeared around the curve of the vertebra, a sleek, heavily armored carriage suspended from the track by ossified clamps. Two guards rode the rear platform, their dark uniforms stark against the pale bone, their hands resting on kinetic clubs. They looked relaxed, accustomed to the safety of the upper tiers.

Kaelen didn't hesitate. As the carriage passed directly over him, he released his grip on the track, dropping onto the transport's roof.

The impact jarred his shoulder, a sickening jolt that tasted like metal in the back of his throat. He stayed flat, letting the carriage's momentum carry him forward. The guards below shouted something, the sound snatched away by the wind. They had heard the impact.

Kaelen slithered to the access hatch. It was locked, secured by a heavy bone-latch. He pressed his knuckles against it. Two taps. Solid density.

He closed his eyes, isolating the feeling of his right radius, and hummed the first note low in his throat. The bone under his hand warmed instantly. He split the chord, forcing a second, sharper tone over the first. The ache hit his arm like a hammer. Calcium leached from his skeleton, bleeding out through his palm and into the latch.

The bone softened, yielding like heated wax. He twisted the latch, silencing the chord with a gasp. His right arm felt terrifyingly light. Brittle. The cost was paid.

He wrenched the hatch open and dropped inside.

The interior of the transport was cold and smelled strongly of ozone. Racks of reinforced glass vials lined the walls, each filled with the thick, glowing liquid of pure marrow-oil. Kaelen snatched three vials, stuffing them into his padded satchel. The weight of them was a physical relief. It meant time for Elara. It meant breath.

The transport shuddered as the hatch above slammed shut.

"You."

Kaelen spun around. A third guard, previously obscured in the forward compartment, stood with an ossified club raised. The weapon hummed, charged with kinetic energy.

Kaelen lunged left as the guard swung. The club smashed into the rack, shattering a dozen vials. The sweet, intense smell of pure marrow flooded the cramped space. Kaelen didn't stop moving. He drove his shoulder into the guard's midsection, using the man's own momentum against him. They crashed into the opposite wall, the transport swaying violently.

Kaelen scrambled up, kicking the hatch open and pulling himself back onto the roof. The two exterior guards were already climbing up the sides of the carriage.

"He's using the low-G frequency! I heard it!" one of them yelled.

Kaelen ran to the edge of the roof and jumped.

He plummeted toward the Ivory Ward's thoroughfare. Below, the bright silk and crystal lamps rushed up to meet him. At the last possible second, he caught the edge of a protruding rib-strut, the sudden stop wrenching his weakened arm. Pain flared, blinding and white-hot.

He hung there for a heartbeat, gasping. The transport screeched to a halt above him, guards shouting, alarms beginning to wail—a high-pitched mechanical shriek that clashed against the ambient hum of the city. They knew his frequency now. They could track him.

Kaelen pulled himself up onto the strut, his breath tearing in his throat. He clutched the satchel to his chest, feeling the heat of the vials through the leather. He had the oil.

He began the long climb down toward the Rib Slums. As he moved, the bone beneath his hands felt unusually warm. It wasn't the ambient heat of the city, nor the friction of the transport track. It was a deep, rhythmic vibration, rising from the darkest parts of the skull and spine.

*Thump. Thump.*

It wasn't the wind. It wasn't the machinery. It almost felt alive. A slow, massive heartbeat, resonating through the entire skeleton of Ostrum. Kaelen ignored it. He had a sister to save, and a debt of bone he could no longer afford to pay.
