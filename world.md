# World Bible

## Cosmology & History
The city of Ostrum was not built; it was carved. It rests entirely within the ribcage and partially sunken skull of a leviathan that died long before the first humans arrived.

**Timeline:**
*   **The Fall (Unknown):** The leviathan, known as the Pale God, collapses in the shallow marrow-swamps of the Ashen Coast.
*   **The Hollow Settlement (300 Years Ago):** Refugees flee the Sunless Empire and find shelter in the ribcage. They discover the marrow inside the bones burns hot and steady.
*   **The First Osteomancer (200 Years Ago):** A marrow-miner discovers that singing at specific frequencies causes the leviathan's bone to become malleable like clay, allowing for rapid expansion.
*   **The Marrow Wars (50 Years Ago):** The noble houses fight for control of the richest marrow veins near the spine. House Vane takes control, establishing the current oligarchy.
*   **Present Day:** Ostrum is a sprawling, vertical metropolis of bone, scaffolding, and marrow-wood. The lower ribs are slums, slowly sinking into the toxic marrow-swamp. The upper spine is where the elite live in carved ivory palaces. The city relies entirely on mining the Pale God's marrow for light, heat, and magic.

## Magic System

### Hard Rules (Osteomancy)
Osteomancers (Bone-singers) can reshape the Pale God's bone by humming two distinct, overlapping notes (a technique called "splitting the chord").
*   **Reshaping:** They can mold bone into weapons, bridges, or housing.
*   **Memory Extraction:** By pressing their ear to a raw marrow vein and singing the "Note of Recall," they can draw out memories stored within the marrow. (The leviathan absorbed the memories of everything that lived and died upon it).

**Costs and Limitations:**
*   **The Brittle Toll:** Every time an Osteomancer shapes bone, a fraction of calcium is drawn from their own skeleton. Frequent use makes their bones fragile.
*   **The Memory Tax:** Extracting a memory from marrow permanently erases a random, equivalent memory from the Osteomancer's own mind. Pulling a complex memory might cost them the memory of their mother's face.
*   **The Shattering:** If an Osteomancer draws too much power, their bones crystallize into a substance called "Shatterglass." A simple fall can break their legs, and eventually, their ribcage will shatter simply from the pressure of breathing.

### Societal Implications
1.  **The Glass Aristocracy:** The ruling elite (House Vane) employ "Shattered" Osteomancers. Because these singers are so fragile, they are carried on palanquins lined with down feathers and treated as living, fragile gods—but they are essentially slaves to the houses, too weak to escape.
2.  **Memory Brokering:** Memories extracted from the marrow are sold as highly addictive experiences. The poor sell their own memories to brokers in exchange for marrow-oil, leading to a subclass of "Hollows"—people who have sold so much of their past they barely remember who they are.
3.  **Physical Deformity:** The poor, who cannot afford clean marrow-oil, burn "slag-marrow," which gives off a toxic fume that causes Marrow-Rot—a disease that blackens the gums and slowly eats away at the joints.

## Geography
Ostrum is vertical, claustrophobic, and divided by altitude. The air smells constantly of salt, old calcium, and the sweet, cloying scent of burning marrow-oil.

*   **The Rib Slums (The Dregs):** The lowest level, resting just above the marrow-swamp. The air is choked with toxic fumes from slag-marrow fires. Travel is via rickety marrow-wood bridges strung between the massive, curving ribs. It is perpetually damp, and the bone is stained gray with soot.
*   **The Spine (The Ivory Ward):** The highest, most pristine area of the city, carved directly into the leviathan's spinal column. Sunlit, airy, and scrubbed white by indentured servants. Travel is via bone-dust elevators powered by wind-catchers.
*   **The Marrow Mines (The Deep Core):** The treacherous tunnels bored directly into the thickest parts of the leviathan's skeleton. It is pitch-black, suffocatingly hot, and reeks of copper and rotting meat. This is where the marrow is harvested.
*   **The Ashen Coast (Outside):** The desolate, gray-sand wasteland surrounding the Pale God. It is swept by brutal salt-storms. Nothing grows here except razor-kelp.

## Factions & Politics
*   **House Vane:** The ruling oligarchy. They control the Deep Core and the distribution of clean marrow-oil. They are ruthless, maintaining power by employing the most powerful (and most brittle) Osteomancers.
*   **The Hollow Syndicate:** A criminal organization operating in the Rib Slums. They deal in illegal memory extraction and smuggling untaxed marrow-oil. They are led by "The Archivist," a woman who has stolen so many memories she speaks in a dozen different voices.
*   **The Marrow-Masons:** The union of miners who work the Deep Core. They are fiercely loyal to one another, deeply superstitious, and currently threatening to strike due to the increasing frequency of "tremors" in the deep bone.
*   **The Cult of the Waking God:** A radical, underground religious sect that believes the leviathan is not dead, but sleeping, and that the continuous mining of marrow is waking it up. They are hunted as heretics by House Vane.

## Bestiary / Flora / Natural World
*   **Marrow-Hounds:** Scavengers that roam the Rib Slums. They look like hairless, pale dogs with elongated snouts designed to suck marrow from discarded bones. They are blind but have acute hearing.
*   **Corpse-Orchids:** The only flower that grows in Ostrum. They sprout exclusively from the marrow-swamp at the city's base. They are a sickly translucent white and smell faintly of vanilla and blood. They are ground into a paste to treat Marrow-Rot, but the supply is strictly controlled by House Vane.
*   **Razor-Kelp:** A hardy, glass-like plant that grows on the Ashen Coast outside the city. The wind blowing through the razor-kelp creates a constant, high-pitched shrieking sound that serves as the background noise of Ostrum.

## Cultural Details
*   **The Tapping:** A common superstition. Whenever a citizen crosses a marrow-wood bridge or enters a new district, they tap the leviathan's bone twice with their knuckles—once out of respect for the Pale God, and once to ensure the bone is solid.
*   **Memory-Wines:** The elite consume memories dissolved in expensive, pale wine. Drinking a "Memory of First Love" or a "Memory of Victory" is a common parlor trick at House Vane parties.
*   **The Festival of the Hollow:** Once a year, the poor paint their faces stark white (to mimic skull-bone) and light massive bonfires of slag-marrow to mourn the memories they have had to sell to survive. House Vane officially ignores this festival but doubles the city guard.
*   **Language quirk:** "Brittle" is a common insult for someone weak or cowardly. "Deep-core" is slang for something genuine or honest.

## Internal Consistency Rules
*   **The Law of Conservation of Mass:** Osteomancers cannot create bone from nothing. To build a wall, they must draw bone mass from somewhere else—either thinning an existing structure (making it dangerous) or pulling from their own body.
*   **The Fragility Principle:** The more powerful the Osteomancer, the more fragile they physically are. The strongest magic-user in the city cannot walk without shattering their femurs.
*   **The Tremors:** The "earthquakes" that occasionally shake Ostrum are NOT tectonic. They are the leviathan shifting in its sleep. (This is a hard fact known to the author, but only suspected by a few characters). The closer a miner gets to the skull, the warmer the bone feels.
