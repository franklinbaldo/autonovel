# World Bible: The Mnemic Wastes and the City of Oakhaven

## Cosmology & History

*   **Year 0: The Sundering of the Mind.** The world was once rich in magic, but a cataclysmic event known as the Sundering shattered the magical leylines. Instead of environmental magic, the residual energy embedded itself into human cognition. Memories became the only conduit for this leftover magic.
*   **Year 12: The Discovery of the Siphon.** A desperate surgeon, Elias Thorne, discovered that cerebrospinal fluid, when treated with silver and belladonna, could suspend and preserve human memories outside the body. He invented the "Siphon"—a brutal needle-and-pump apparatus that extracts the fluid.
*   **Year 45: The Establishment of the Mnemic Standard.** Oakhaven, the largest surviving settlement, shifted its entire economy. Gold and silver lost value. Memories—specifically, joyful, innocent, or powerful ones—became the only accepted currency for food, shelter, and survival.
*   **Year 98: The Hollow Riots.** A massive uprising by the "Hollows" (those who had sold so many memories they could barely function) resulted in the burning of the Lower Wards. The memory-rich elite responded by forming the Cortex Guard to brutally suppress future uprisings.
*   **Year 142 (Present Day): The Dregs Crisis.** The supply of pure, high-value memories is dwindling as the population becomes increasingly hollowed out. A black market has surged for "dregs" (diluted, fractured, or traumatic memories), and a new, mysterious illness called "The Fade" is spreading among those who consume too many extracted memories.

## Magic System

### Hard Rules (Extraction and Consumption)

*   **The Law of Conservation:** A memory cannot be duplicated, only transferred. Once extracted, it is gone from the donor's mind forever. It leaves a "blank spot"—a physical sensation of coldness or missing time in the donor's head.
*   **The Cost of Extraction (Donor):** The physical process is excruciating. It requires a puncture at the base of the skull. Infection is common. Repeated extractions lead to "Hollowing"—a progressive loss of personality, motor skills, and eventually, the will to live. Donors often suffer from Phantom Recollection—aching for a memory they know they lost but cannot identify.
*   **The Cost of Consumption (Buyer):** Consuming a memory involves ingesting the treated fluid. It provides a rush of the donor's experience and emotion. However, the buyer's brain struggles to integrate foreign memories. Consuming too many leads to "The Fade"—a state where the buyer can no longer distinguish their own memories from the ones they purchased, leading to violent psychosis or catatonia.
*   **Decay:** Extracted memories are volatile. Even in sealed glass vials, they degrade over time, turning from a warm amber (high quality) to a cloudy grey (worthless "dregs").

### Soft Magic (The Whisperers)

*   A rare mutation has appeared among the children of the Hollows. These "Whisperers" can hear the faint echoes of memories trapped in the residual fluid on used Siphons or empty vials. They cannot extract or consume, but they can sense the emotional weight and general subject of a lost memory. The elite fear them; the black market uses them as appraisers.

### Societal Implications

*   **Class Structure:** Society is strictly divided. The "Cortex Elite" live in the Upper Wards, bloated with purchased memories, living multiple lifetimes of joy and experience. The "Hollows" live in the sludge of the Lower Wards, surviving by selling their childhoods piece by piece just to buy bread.
*   **Crime and Punishment:** The worst punishment in Oakhaven is the "Complete Siphon." Criminals have their entire lifetimes extracted, leaving them as empty, drooling husks. These husks are used for menial, dangerous labor in the mines. The stolen memories are sold at state auction.
*   **Family Life among the Poor:** Having a child is both a burden and an investment. A child's pure, untainted memories (a first laugh, a sunny afternoon) fetch the highest prices on the market. Families often face the agonizing choice of letting their children starve or selling their children's happiness to feed them.

## Geography

*   **Oakhaven:** A city built into the walls of a massive, dried-up crater. The architecture is brutalist stone and rusted iron. The air always smells of coal smoke, damp earth, and the sharp, ozone tang of memory fluid.
*   **The Upper Wards (The Crown):** Located at the rim of the crater. Sunlit, with hanging gardens and guarded gates. The streets are paved with white stone. The air smells of imported spices and clean linen.
*   **The Lower Wards (The Basin):** Located at the bottom of the crater, a slum of corrugated tin and rotting wood. It is constantly shadowed. A thick, grey fog—a byproduct of the crude refineries that process memory fluid—hangs in the air. It smells of sewage, rust, and desperation.
*   **The Rust Barrens:** The wasteland outside the crater. Miles of jagged, rusted metal spikes protruding from cracked red earth. Toxic dust storms make travel nearly impossible without heavy protective gear. It is where the Hollows who can no longer work are exiled.

## Factions & Politics

*   **The Cortex Elite:** The wealthy merchants and politicians who control the legal memory banks. They want to maintain the current economic system and strictly regulate extraction to keep prices high.
*   **The Siphon Cartels:** Black market operators in the Lower Wards. They perform illegal, unsafe extractions and deal in "dregs" and stolen memories. They want to break the Elite's monopoly.
*   **The Blank Slate:** A radical, violent cult of Hollows and Whisperers. They believe that all memory extraction is a sin against nature. Their goal is to destroy the memory banks and release all stored memories into the air, forcing everyone to start over from nothing.
*   **The Enforcers (The Grey Coats):** The city's brutal police force. They are paid in high-quality combat and tactical memories, making them unnaturally skilled fighters. They are loyal only to whoever controls the memory vaults.

## Cultural Details

*   **The Festival of the Empty Cup:** A grim, yearly holiday in the Lower Wards where people gather to drink cheap alcohol and try to guess what memories they have lost. It usually ends in weeping and brawls.
*   **Vial Etiquette:** In the Upper Wards, it is considered highly offensive to ask the origin of a consumed memory at a dinner party. It is treated like discussing bowel movements.
*   **Clothing:** The wealthy wear pale, easily stained colors (whites, creams) to show they don't work in the soot of the Lower Wards. The poor wear heavy, dark canvas that hides the stains of blood and memory fluid.
*   **The Taboo of Writing:** Because memory is commodified, keeping written journals is highly illegal for anyone outside the Cortex Elite. Writing down a memory makes it harder to forget when extracted, causing complications in the extraction process. The Enforcers burn any books they find in the Lower Wards.

## Internal Consistency Rules

*   **No Magic Fireballs:** The only magic is the extraction, preservation, and consumption of memories via the physical fluid. No spells, no telekinesis.
*   **The Fluid is Physical:** Memory fluid obeys gravity, evaporates if not sealed, and can be contaminated by dirt or blood. It is a tangible substance.
*   **Extraction Requires Consent or Restraint:** A memory cannot be extracted from a moving, fighting target. The donor must be perfectly still (either willing, drugged, or heavily restrained) for the Siphon to hit the exact spot at the base of the skull without causing instant death.
