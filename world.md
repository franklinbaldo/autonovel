# World Bible

## Cosmology & History
The founding myth says the world was forged in light and noise, a great chaotic thought. The first mages were called The Rememberers, able to call forth the physical shapes of their past. But they didn't know the cost until their minds turned white as bleached bone.

Three hundred years ago, the War of Ash began. The continent was engulfed by armies burning entire lineages to fuel destruction. Cities fell not to swords but to sudden storms and earthquakes paid for by soldiers forgetting their wives and children. The War ended abruptly when the commanders woke up and could not recognize each other, could not remember why they were fighting.

The present day is built on the ruins of that war. Memory is currency, weapon, and tool. The rich hoard their experiences in quiet, walled gardens to build strong spells; the poor burn their childhoods to light a stove or heal a broken leg. The tension between those with full lives and the "Hollowed" (those who have burned too much) drives the politics of the capital, Oakhaven.

## Magic System
### Hard Rules (NEVER violate)
- **The Currency:** Magic (called "Kindling") is performed by offering a memory to the air.
- **The Cost:** The memory is permanently erased from the caster's mind. The stronger the emotion attached to the memory, the more powerful the spell. A favorite meal creates a small spark; the birth of a child can level a city block.
- **The Limit:** You cannot burn what you do not truly own. You cannot burn a fact learned from a book, only a lived experience. You cannot burn someone else's memory.
- **The Feedback Loop:** As casters burn memories, the emotions associated with them vanish. Burning a memory of love removes the capacity to feel that specific love. Over time, casters become cold, pragmatic, and detached.

### Soft Rules (can bend for story)
- **The Ash:** When a memory is burned, a physical residue is left behind—a faint scent of ozone and something sweet, like old paper. Strong spells leave a visible grey ash.
- **The Echo:** Sometimes, a burned memory leaves a "ghost shape" in the mind. A person might know they *should* feel sad about a dead parent, but the feeling itself is gone. This causes severe psychological distress.
- **The Anchor:** Some casters use physical objects (a locket, a carved toy) as "Anchors" to try and hold onto the shape of a burned memory. The object remains, but its meaning is hollowed out.

### Societal Implications
1. **The Economy of Experience:** Experiences are commodities. The wealthy hire "Makers" to choreograph elaborate, emotionally intense days for them, simply so they have high-quality fuel to burn later.
2. **The Hollowed:** The underclass consists of people who have burned too much of their lives to survive. They wander the streets, performing repetitive tasks from trades they no longer understand. They are treated with fear and pity.
3. **The Law:** It is illegal to force another person to cast a spell under duress (effectively stealing their life). The punishment is "The White Room," solitary confinement designed to offer zero new experiences, starving the prisoner of magical fuel.

## Geography
**Oakhaven (The Capital):** A city built in the crater left by the War of Ash. It smells constantly of damp stone and the sweet ozone of minor spells. The inner ring is the Ward of Glass, where the wealthy live in sensory-rich environments (bright colors, loud music, sharp spices) to generate strong memories.

**The Slips:** The outer ring of Oakhaven. Muddy, grey, and quiet. People speak in low voices to avoid drawing attention. The dominant smell is boiled cabbage and wet wool. This is where the Hollowed gather.

**The Bleached Wastes:** The desert beyond the city, created during the War. The sand is actually the compressed ash of millions of burned memories. It is completely silent. Travel across it is dangerous because the sheer lack of sensory input makes it impossible to generate new fuel for survival spells.

## Factions & Politics
- **The Keepers:** The ruling class of Oakhaven. They believe magic should be heavily regulated and only used by licensed state actors. They hoard their own memories and live long, rich lives.
- **The Cinder Syndicate:** A black-market organization that deals in illegal experiences. They run underground fight clubs, drug dens, and sensory parlors designed to generate intense, desperate memories for quick, cheap magical fuel.
- **The Archivists:** A religious order that believes burning any memory is a sin against the Creator. They write everything down, covering their bodies in tattoos of their own histories so they can never forget, even if they are forced to burn the memory itself.

## Bestiary / Flora
- **Ash Moths:** Small, grey insects that feed on the residue left by spells. A swarm of them indicates recent magic use.
- **The Whisper Willow:** A tree that grows in the Slips. Its leaves rustle even when there is no wind, and the sound is said to mimic the voices of the Hollowed trying to remember their names.

## Cultural Details
- **The Ledger:** Every citizen carries a small, bound book where they write down the events of their day. It is a desperate attempt to keep a record of what they might have to burn tomorrow.
- **The Taboo of Nostalgia:** Speaking fondly of the past is considered vulgar and dangerous, as it reveals what memories you value most (and therefore, your most potent magical fuel).
- **The Diet:** Food in the Slips is intentionally bland (porridge, root vegetables). Strong flavors are saved for when you need to generate a quick, minor memory to light a fire.

## Internal Consistency Rules
- A character cannot suddenly remember something they burned. It is gone forever.
- A spell's effect must be proportional to the emotional weight of the memory burned.
- A character who has burned all memories of a person will treat that person as a complete stranger, even if they are holding a photograph of them together.
